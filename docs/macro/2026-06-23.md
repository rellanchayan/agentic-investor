# Market Weather — 2026-06-23

## Posture: CAUTIOUS

**One-sentence reason:** A global tech and chip selloff is pushing US futures sharply lower this morning while the market remains expensive and a key inflation report lands later this week — demand a wider discount before any new purchase.

---

## What the key terms mean (quick reference)

- **SPY**: the fund holding the 500 largest US companies. It is our benchmark — "the market."
- **200-day average**: SPY's average price over the last ~200 trading days. Price above it = broad uptrend; below = downtrend.
- **Fed / interest rates**: the US central bank sets short-term borrowing costs. Higher rates make debt more expensive and often push stock prices down.
- **PCE**: Personal Consumption Expenditures index — the Fed's preferred measure of inflation. A hot print can push rates higher and spook stocks.

---

## SPY trend

SPY closed at **$737.11**, which is **above** its 200-day average of **$689.28** (a gap of ~6.9%). The long-run trend is still technically an uptrend. However, the market has rallied far and fast from its lows, and valuations are stretched.

---

## What is happening in markets right now

US stock futures are under significant pressure this morning (Tuesday Jun 23):

- S&P 500 futures down roughly **1–2%** ahead of the open.
- Nasdaq futures down roughly **2–3%**, with technology stocks leading the drop.

The selloff started overnight in Asia. South Korea's Kospi index fell more than **10%** in a single session — severe enough to trigger a circuit-breaker trading halt — as chip giants Samsung and SK Hynix each lost more than 12%. Japan's Nikkei fell more than 3%.

The drop then spread to Europe and is now hitting US pre-market trading.

**What is driving it:**

1. **Hawkish Fed repricing.** Under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh (who took over from Jerome Powell), markets are now pricing in roughly **50 basis points of rate hikes** by December 2026 — double what was expected just two weeks ago. The Fed held rates steady at 3.50%–3.75% at its June 17 meeting, but the tone was more hawkish than expected.

2. **AI and chip valuation doubts.** Chip and AI-linked stocks had an enormous run. Investors are now questioning whether the debt-funded AI infrastructure buildout can be justified at current valuations if rates stay high longer. Nvidia fell ~3%, Micron and SanDisk each fell 12–13%, and several chipmakers fell 6–9%.

3. **PCE inflation report due Thursday.** The Bureau of Economic Analysis releases the PCE inflation index on **Thursday June 25 at 8:30am ET**. This is the Fed's most-watched inflation gauge. A hot reading would reinforce the case for more rate hikes.

---

## Market valuation

The S&P 500 price-to-earnings (PE) ratio stands at approximately **25–32x** depending on the methodology (trailing vs. forward earnings). The long-run historical median is around **18x**. By most measures the market is **expensive** — trading in roughly the 90th percentile of recent valuations. This is not a reason to panic or sell, but it does mean good companies need to be purchased with a meaningful discount to be worth buying.

---

## Rates

The 10-year Treasury yield is near **4.48%** — elevated, and rising expectations are pushing it higher. Higher long-term rates put downward pressure on growth stock valuations (the ones with earnings far in the future, like many tech names). The Fed held steady in June but the new leadership has signalled it is willing to hike again if inflation does not cool.

## What this means for us today

We hold **$1,000,000 in cash** and have **no current positions**. The cautious posture means:

- Do not lower our bar to chase names during the selloff. A falling price does not automatically make a name cheap enough.
- Any buy must clear a **wider margin of safety** than on a normal day — the market is expensive overall and a live catalyst (PCE on Thursday) could add more volatility.
- If a high-conviction name drops to a genuinely attractive price (well below our base fair value, not just at it), that is still actionable — but the bar is higher today.
- The PLAYBOOK principle applies: "A missed buy costs nothing; an overpriced buy costs money. When in doubt, HOLD and keep cash."
- Thursday's PCE print may clarify the rate picture. If inflation is cooling, the market could bounce and we may see a better entry. If it is hot, more selling is possible and patience pays.
