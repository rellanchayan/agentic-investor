# Market Weather — 2026-06-24

## Posture: CAUTIOUS

**One-sentence reason:** The market is trading at historically stretched valuations (Shiller CAPE near the 98th percentile), the Fed is signaling a possible rate hike rather than a cut, and the Fed's preferred inflation gauge (PCE) drops Thursday — together these warrant demanding a larger-than-normal discount before buying anything.

---

## What the numbers say

**SPY (our benchmark):** Closed at $737.62 yesterday, which is about 7% above its 200-day average of $689.71. Being above the 200-day average means the broad market is in an uptrend — that is good. But "uptrend" does not mean "cheap." We need both.

**Valuation (how expensive is the market?):**
- The Shiller CAPE ratio — a smoothed, inflation-adjusted price-to-earnings measure that is hard to game — sits around 41 as of June 2026. The long-run historical average is about 17–18. The current reading is at roughly the 98th percentile of all history, meaning the market has been cheaper than this about 98% of the time. By this measure, the market is expensive.
- The simpler trailing P/E ratio is in the 25–32 range depending on the source, also above the long-run median of about 18.

**Interest rates / the Fed:**
- The Federal Open Market Committee met on June 16–17 and voted 12-0 to hold short-term rates steady at 3.50–3.75%. This is the fourth consecutive hold.
- The important shift: the Fed's "dot plot" (each official's forecast for where rates should go) now tilts toward a possible hike rather than the cuts that were expected earlier this year. Nine officials see at least one hike this year. This is a meaningful change in tone.
- The 10-year Treasury yield is around 4.50%.

**Inflation:**
- The Fed revised its PCE inflation forecast up to 3.6% for 2026, well above its 2% target. Core PCE (which strips out food and energy) is running around 3.3%. Elevated inflation gives the Fed reason to keep rates higher for longer, which puts a ceiling on how high stock prices can go.

**Futures this morning (June 24):**
- S&P 500 futures are pointing modestly higher, up about 0.13%, as investors look for bargains after two consecutive down sessions driven by a sell-off in AI and semiconductor stocks. A 71% probability on prediction markets says the index opens higher today.
- This bounce looks like normal short-term noise after a tech pullback, not a change in the underlying macro picture.

**Key event this week:**
- **Thursday, June 26, 8:30am ET — PCE inflation data (May).** This is the single most important data point of the week. It is the Fed's preferred measure of inflation. If it comes in hotter than expected, the market could sell off as traders price in an even higher chance of a rate hike. If it comes in cool, markets could rally.

## What this means for us today

We hold $1,000,000 in cash with no positions. The cautious posture means we should not rush to put money to work simply because there is cash sitting idle. Before buying any name, we need to see a meaningful margin of safety — the price should be well below our estimated fair value, not just a little below. A market trading at the 98th historical CAPE percentile with the Fed potentially raising rates is not the time to settle for a thin discount.

Patience is the right posture here. When the right company falls to the right price, we buy a small amount (our daily $5,000 budget keeps us disciplined). Until then, cash is not a mistake — it is dry powder waiting for a better pitch.
