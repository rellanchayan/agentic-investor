# Market Weather — 2026-07-06

**Posture: CAUTIOUS**

The market is not in freefall — SPY is in a healthy structural uptrend — but two near-term events this week and next make this a moment to hold cash and demand a bigger discount than usual before buying anything.

---

## What the Market Is Doing Right Now

**SPY** (our benchmark fund that holds the 500 largest US companies) closed last week at $749.77. Its **200-day average** — the average price over the last ~200 trading days, which tells us whether the market is in a long-term uptrend or downtrend — is $692.71. SPY is 8.2% *above* that average. That is a bullish signal: the broad market is in an uptrend, not a collapse.

This is the first trading day after the July 4 holiday weekend. S&P 500 futures were up about 0.45% in pre-market, pointing to a slightly positive open. The Dow Jones recently hit a fresh all-time record above 53,000. On the surface, the mood is calm and constructive.

---

## Why We Are Cautious Anyway

### 1. A Tariff Deadline Is 3 Days Away

On **Thursday, July 9**, the 90-day pause on higher US reciprocal tariffs expires. Unless the administration grants another extension, tariff rates on imports from many countries snap back to higher levels (11-50% range, depending on the country). As of this Monday morning, no extension has been announced for this round.

Markets have grown comfortable assuming these deadlines get kicked down the road — but that assumption could be wrong. A surprise snap-back would raise costs for US companies, reignite inflation, and likely cause a sharp selloff. This is a coin-flip headline risk we cannot price around.

(A separate tariff — the 10% global import duty under Section 122 — expires on July 24. Two tariff cliffs in one month.)

### 2. Inflation Is Still Running Hot

The **CPI** (Consumer Price Index — the government's main measure of how fast prices are rising) for May came in at 4.2% year-over-year. The **Fed's target** is 2%. We are more than double the target.

The next CPI reading (for June) comes out on **Tuesday, July 14**, at 8:30 AM Eastern. If it is still hot, the Fed will feel no pressure to cut rates, and the market could reprice downward. If it surprises lower, that would be a tailwind. Either way, it is a market-moving event arriving in 8 days.

### 3. The Fed Is Holding Rates High With No Relief in Sight

The **Federal Reserve** (the US central bank) sets short-term interest rates. Higher rates make borrowing more expensive and tend to push stock prices down. The current rate is 3.5-3.75%, held steady for four consecutive meetings. The next Fed meeting is July 28-29, and markets are pricing in an 89% probability of another hold — no cut.

The new Fed Chair, Kevin Warsh, has communicated in a "hawkish" (tight-money) tone and has reduced the amount of advance guidance the Fed gives. That uncertainty itself is a mild headwind.

### 4. The Jobs Market Just Surprised to the Downside

The **jobs report** for June (released July 2) showed only **57,000 new jobs** added — well below the 115,000 the market expected. The prior two months were revised down by a combined 74,000. This is a sign the labor market is slowing. That could eventually push the Fed toward cuts, but for now it just adds uncertainty: slower growth + still-hot inflation is an uncomfortable combination.

### 5. The Market Is Not Cheap

The **forward P/E ratio** (what investors are paying per dollar of expected earnings) is about **20.4** — above the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. The Shiller CAPE ratio (a longer-run valuation measure) is around **39.9**, which is roughly 125% above its long-run historical average. By most measures, US stocks are expensive, not on sale.

Looking at our own watchlist: every name we have a fair-value estimate for is currently priced *above* our estimate:
- **AAPL**: trading at $311.67 vs. our base fair value of ~$195 — 60% above what we think it is worth
- **V (Visa)**: trading at $358.17 vs. our base fair value of ~$297 — 21% above fair value
- **MSFT**: trading at $384.14 vs. our base fair value of ~$378 — essentially at fair value, no discount

None of these pass our margin-of-safety test today.

---

## What Is Working in the Market's Favor

- **The trend is up.** SPY is solidly above its 200-day average. The market has not broken down.
- **Oil prices are down ~30%** from their May peak (around $69/barrel), which reduces energy inflation and eases cost pressure on businesses.
- **Earnings are strong.** Q2 2026 corporate earnings are tracking roughly 23% above last year — companies are making money.
- **Futures are green this morning**, suggesting the holiday mood is positive and no overnight shock occurred.

---

## What This Means for Us Today

The posture is **CAUTIOUS**. In practice that means:

1. We do not rush to buy just because the market is up and the trend is intact.
2. We require a *larger-than-normal* margin of safety before placing any order. Our standard threshold should be raised by roughly 5 percentage points in cautious mode — good companies need to be meaningfully on sale, not just at fair value.
3. The tariff deadline on July 9 is the most immediate wildcard. If tariffs are extended (kick the can), sentiment likely improves. If tariffs snap back, we may see a pullback that actually creates buying opportunities. Sitting in cash preserves optionality either way.
4. We watch for the June CPI report on July 14. A cooler-than-expected reading could shift the posture toward normal next week.

Cash is not a mistake today. It is the disciplined choice given elevated valuations, no names meeting our threshold, and a live tariff catalyst 72 hours away.
