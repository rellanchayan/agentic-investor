**What earnings season means:** Several times a year, publicly traded companies report their quarterly profits. This is one of those periods. Late July is when many of the largest technology companies report for the April-June quarter. When a big company misses expectations, its stock can drop sharply in a single day. When it beats, it can jump. This creates more volatility than usual — prices move more, and the moves can be large and sudden. For a long-term investor, earnings-driven moves are usually noise, but they are a reason to be careful about limit prices on any buy orders, because the stock might gap in either direction overnight.
