# Market Weather — 2026-07-21

## Posture: CAUTIOUS

**One-sentence reason:** The market is in a structural uptrend and fear is moderate, but valuation is stretched above historical averages, CPI inflation is running at 4.2% (well above the Fed's 2% target), the FOMC meets in eight days with a non-trivial rate-hike risk, Iran-US tensions remain unresolved, and a dense earnings week introduces near-term volatility — no thesis candidate offers a sufficient discount at current prices.

---

## What the Benchmarks Say

- **SPY** (the 500 largest US companies) closed at **746.79**, which is **7.1% above its 200-day average of 697.44**. That means the broad market is in an uptrend — not a moment of panic or crash.
- **VIX** (the "fear gauge" — measures how nervous options traders are): closed at 18.65 Monday, then fell to roughly **17.33** in early Tuesday trading as ceasefire diplomacy cooled oil prices. A VIX of 17-18 is moderate, not elevated. The market is not panicking.
- **S&P 500 futures** are up about **0.45-0.49%** this morning (Tuesday), led by tech and semiconductor stocks. Asian markets provided a tailwind, with South Korea's KOSPI surging over 3%.

---

## Interest Rates and the Fed

- The Federal Reserve's current target rate is **3.50%–3.75%**, set under new Chair Kevin Warsh after the June 2026 meeting.
- The **10-year Treasury yield** is approximately **4.59%** — still elevated relative to the last few years, which keeps borrowing costs high for companies and competes with stocks for investor dollars.
- The next **FOMC meeting is July 28-29**, with the rate decision announced **Wednesday July 29 at 2:00 PM ET**. The Fed is now in its pre-meeting "blackout period" (officials stop speaking publicly). Markets assign roughly **92% probability to no change** and **~7% to a 25 basis-point hike**. A hike is not the base case, but it is not off the table.
- No Summary of Economic Projections (the "dot plot") will be released at this meeting — less clarity on the Fed's forward path.

---

## Inflation

- The most recent **CPI reading (May 2026) showed inflation at 4.2% year-over-year**, driven by a 23.5% surge in energy prices tied to Middle East tensions. That is more than double the Fed's 2% target.
- Elevated inflation keeps the door open to rate hikes and reduces the case for cuts this year. This is the single most important macro headwind right now.

---

## Geopolitics and Oil

- After the Iran-US ceasefire broke down in early July (President Trump declared it "over"), there were ten consecutive nights of US airstrikes on Iran. Crude oil rose to near **$90 per barrel**, its highest level in months.
- As of this morning, mediators are pushing for a new **10-day ceasefire**, and early diplomatic signals are helping oil prices edge lower — which is why futures are green today.
- The situation remains fragile. A renewed escalation would push oil higher, feed inflation, and give the Fed reason to hike.
- Separately, President Trump has imposed **50% tariffs on Canadian goods** (dairy and chemicals, effective in 30 days; Canadian oil is exempt). This adds another layer of macro uncertainty.

---

## Valuation

- The **forward 12-month P/E ratio for the S&P 500 is approximately 20.3x**. That is above the 5-year average (19.9x) and the 10-year average (19.0x). The trailing P/E is ~25.4x versus a historical median of 18.0x.
- In plain terms: the market is not screamingly expensive, but it is not cheap either. A patient investor should demand a bigger discount before committing cash.

## What This Means for Us Today

We are long-term investors, so no single week's earnings or one FOMC meeting should cause panic. But the right response to today's environment is to be patient and demanding:

1. **Do not lower the margin-of-safety bar.** The three names with live theses (AAPL, MSFT, V) are all trading above their base fair values. AAPL is deeply overvalued relative to our thesis. MSFT is closest but still 5.8% above our base fair value. None qualify for a buy today.
2. **The FOMC meeting in 8 days is a real uncertainty.** An unexpected hike (7% probability) could cause a broad market pullback. Holding cash means we would be better positioned to buy quality names at a discount after such a move.
3. **Earnings volatility this week** (especially Alphabet on Wednesday) could create short-term price swings in tech names we watch. Watch but do not chase.
4. **Hold all cash.** The cautious posture is earned — not a reflex from the prior week. A written review happened today. The decision to hold is deliberate.
