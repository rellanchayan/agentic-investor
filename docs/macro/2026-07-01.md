# Market Weather — 2026-07-01

**Posture: CAUTIOUS**

**One-sentence reason:** The market just finished its best quarter since 2020 (+14% for the S&P 500), leaving valuations stretched at a trailing P/E of ~25 and a CAPE of ~40 — well above historical averages — while inflation remains above the Fed's 2% target and tariff policy is actively unsettled heading into a holiday-shortened week.

---

## What the Gauges Say

### SPY and the 200-day average
SPY closed yesterday at $746.00. Its 200-day moving average is $691.84. That means SPY is trading about 7.8% above its long-run trend line — a clear uptrend. In plain terms: the broad market has been climbing and is not in distress. That is a tailwind for stock prices in general.

However, a 7.8% premium to the 200-day average means we are buying into strength, not into weakness. A patient investor waits for good prices; we do not chase a rally.

### Interest rates and the Fed
The Federal Reserve held its benchmark interest rate steady at 3.50%–3.75% at the June 17 meeting. This is the fourth consecutive hold. Inflation is described by the Fed as "elevated relative to the 2 percent goal," partly because of supply disruptions and tariff pass-through in prices.

The next Fed decision is July 29. Market odds are roughly 80% for no change and about 20% for a 25-basis-point rate hike. No cut is on the table.

Rates at 3.50%–3.75% are meaningfully above the near-zero levels of 2020–2021. That compresses the premium you should pay for stocks relative to those years.

### Valuation
The S&P 500's trailing P/E ratio is approximately 25 (earnings for the past 12 months). The cyclically-adjusted P/E (CAPE or Shiller P/E), which smooths earnings over 10 years, is approximately 39.9 — about 125% above its long-run historical average. The forward P/E (using expected future earnings) is around 20.

In plain terms: the market is not wildly expensive on forward earnings, but the longer-term view says stocks are priced for a lot of good news. There is not much cushion if earnings disappoint or rates rise.

### This morning's futures
S&P 500 futures are down about 0.2% and Nasdaq futures are down about 0.8% before the open. This is a mild pullback after yesterday wrapped up the best quarter since 2020. Not a panic, just a pause.

### Today's data: ISM Manufacturing
The ISM Manufacturing PMI for June 2026 was released at 10:00am ET today at 53.3. A reading above 50 means expansion; 53.3 means manufacturing is growing at a solid pace. New orders came in at 56.0. This is the 20th consecutive month of manufacturing expansion. This is a genuine tailwind: the economy is not rolling over.

## Macro Risks on the Radar

1. **Tariff escalation.** The administration's Section 122 universal 10% tariff authority expires July 24. The White House has signaled additional tariffs — 50% on copper and 50% on Brazilian exports — coming in August. Tariff costs are already a 5-point headwind to EPS growth for some major consumer-goods companies. This is a live and evolving risk.

2. **Inflation above target.** The Fed is not yet satisfied. If the jobs report Thursday shows the economy running hot, it strengthens the case for a rate hike at the July 29 meeting (currently only ~20% priced).

3. **Stretched valuations after a +14% Q2.** The rally has already priced in a lot of good news. Earnings season kicks off in mid-July and will test whether companies can deliver on elevated expectations. Q2 earnings are projected to grow ~23% year-over-year — a high bar.

4. **Holiday-shortened week.** Friday is a closed market. Any bad news from Thursday's jobs report cannot be traded until Monday July 7. That means weekend headline risk is elevated.

## Tailwinds Worth Noting

1. **Economy expanding.** ISM at 53.3, 20th consecutive month of manufacturing expansion. GDP tracking positive. Not a recession.
2. **Strong earnings growth.** S&P 500 companies are projected to grow earnings ~23% in Q2 2026, with more companies issuing positive guidance than negative — well above the 5-year average.
3. **Market uptrend intact.** SPY is 7.8% above its 200-day average. The long-term trend is up.
4. **Fed on hold.** No rate hike is imminent. 80% odds of no change in July. That is not a headwind.

## What This Means for Us Today

We hold 100% cash. No positions exist. The market is in an uptrend but expensive after a strong quarter. The biggest market event of the week (June jobs report) hits tomorrow morning, and markets are closed Friday — meaning any shock has a long weekend to fester before we can react. 

Our posture is **cautious**: we require a genuine margin of safety before buying, not just a fair price. In practice, this means we want to buy quality names at a meaningful discount to our base fair value estimate. Names trading above our fair value (AAPL at $294 vs. a $195 base, V at $348 vs. a $297 base) do not qualify today regardless of long-term quality.

We do not panic about daily wiggles, and we do not need to deploy cash before the holiday weekend. Patience is the correct call when prices are above intrinsic value.
