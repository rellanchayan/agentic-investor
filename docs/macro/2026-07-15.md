## Background terms for new readers

- **SPY**: a fund that tracks the 500 largest US companies. It is our benchmark.
- **200-day average**: the average SPY price over roughly the last 200 trading sessions. When SPY is above it, the broad market is in an uptrend. When below, a downtrend.
- **Forward P/E**: how much investors are paying today for every dollar of expected earnings over the next year. Higher = more expensive. The 10-year average for the S&P 500 is about 19x earnings.
- **CPI (Consumer Price Index)**: the government's main measure of inflation — how fast everyday prices are rising. The Fed wants it at 2% per year.
- **PPI (Producer Price Index)**: a companion to CPI, measuring price changes at the wholesale/factory level. It often leads where CPI goes next.
- **FOMC**: the Federal Reserve's rate-setting committee. It meets every six to eight weeks and decides whether to raise, cut, or hold short-term interest rates. Higher rates make borrowing more expensive and tend to put downward pressure on stock prices.
- **Fed Chair Warsh**: Kevin Warsh became Fed Chair in 2026. His public testimony can move markets if his words surprise traders about the direction of rates.

### Geopolitical risk — US-Iran conflict

This is the biggest wild card. The US-Iran ceasefire broke down in early July. US military strikes on Iran resumed, and both sides have traded attacks near the Strait of Hormuz. Brent crude oil is running at roughly $84-86 per barrel. This matters because:

1. About 20% of the world's daily oil supply passes through the Strait of Hormuz. Any closure or sustained disruption would spike energy prices sharply.
2. Higher energy prices mean higher CPI in coming months — potentially reversing the progress seen in yesterday's report.
3. The range of outcomes is wide: Goldman Sachs forecasts $80/barrel for Q4 if Persian Gulf exports normalize; BlackRock's CEO has warned of $150/barrel if conflict escalates, or $40 if a genuine peace deal is reached.

The situation is unresolved and constitutes a real, live tail risk.
