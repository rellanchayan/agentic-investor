# Eli Lilly (LLY) — Research Thesis

*As of 2026-Q1 data | Written 2026-06-27 | Refresh due 2026-09-25 | Conviction 3/5 | Data quality: filing-backed*

## What the company does (plain English)
Eli Lilly makes and sells branded prescription medicines. It earns money by selling
patent-protected drugs at very high margins. Today its growth is dominated by its
"incretin" franchise — tirzepatide — sold as **Mounjaro** (type-2 diabetes) and
**Zepbound** (obesity). Other meaningful drugs: **Verzenio** (breast cancer),
**Jardiance** (diabetes/heart, partnered), **Taltz** (immunology), **Trulicity** (older
diabetes GLP-1), and a legacy insulin portfolio. The whole story turns on the
obesity/diabetes wave, where Lilly and Novo Nordisk are the two clear leaders.

## Why it has a moat (rated: WIDE)
- **Patents/IP** on tirzepatide and pipeline molecules.
- A **multi-year manufacturing capacity lead** in incretins — hard for rivals to copy fast.
- **Deep R&D** and a track record of successful Phase 3 readouts.
- **Trusted brand** with physicians and payers, plus high regulatory barriers to entry.

## Fair value band: ~$771 (low) / ~$993 (base) / ~$1,275 (high)
Current price **$1,206.57** (Alpaca paper feed, 2026-06-27).

Method = blend of forward P/E and TTM FCF-yield:
- **Forward P/E:** FY2026 non-GAAP EPS midpoint $36.25 at 26x / 32x / 40x. Premium
  multiples are justified by 25%+ growth, 80%+ gross margin and a wide moat.
- **FCF yield:** TTM FCF/share ~$13.20 at 2.2% / 1.6% / 1.2%. FCF currently trails
  earnings because Lilly is spending heavily on manufacturing capex, so this method
  intentionally pulls the band lower for humility.

**Conclusion:** the base (~$993) sits *below* the current price. LLY is priced for
continued exceptional growth and offers little or negative margin of safety today. A
wonderful business — but not obviously cheap. Hence conviction 3, not higher.

## The real risks
- **Rich valuation** — above base fair value; the thesis needs years of continued growth to pay off.
- **FCF << earnings** — capex-heavy; if growth disappoints, the cash-conversion gap bites.
- **Single-molecule concentration** — tirzepatide drives most of the growth.
- **Competition & pricing** — Novo Nordisk and next-gen/oral rivals; Q1 2026 already showed lower realized prices.
- **Drug-pricing policy** — Medicare negotiation, payer/PBM decisions.
- **Long-term patent cliff** — incretin patents will expire; pipeline must replace them.

## Sell triggers (what would break the thesis)
1. Gross margin below 70% for two consecutive quarters.
2. Total revenue declines YoY for two consecutive quarters.
3. Net debt / EBITDA rises above 3.0x.
4. Material safety signal, FDA label restriction/black-box, or recall on tirzepatide.
5. ROIC falls below 10% on a full-year basis.
6. Free cash flow turns negative for a full fiscal year without a clear temporary capex reason.
7. A serious accounting red flag or restatement.

## Catalysts to watch
- Orforglipron (oral GLP-1) regulatory decisions — potential U.S. obesity action in 2026. ([IR](https://investor.lilly.com/news-releases/news-release-details/lillys-oral-glp-1-orforglipron-successful-third-phase-3-trial))
- Continued Mounjaro/Zepbound volume growth and new capacity coming online.
- FY2026 guidance execution and quarterly beats/raises.
- Label expansions (cardiovascular, sleep apnea, MASH) broadening reimbursement.
