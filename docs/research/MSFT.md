# Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) — Research Thesis

**Thesis ID:** MSFT-2025Q4 | **As of:** FY2025 (ended 2025-06-30) | **Retrieved:** 2026-06-16
**Price (Alpaca):** $393.54 | **Conviction:** 4 / 5 | **Data quality:** filing-backed

## What the business does (plain English)
Microsoft sells software, cloud computing, and devices to businesses, governments, and consumers.
It makes money in three parts:
- **Productivity & Business Processes** — Microsoft 365 (Office), Teams, LinkedIn, Dynamics. **$120.8B** in FY2025.
- **Intelligent Cloud** — Azure (renting computing power over the internet), server software, GitHub. **$106.3B** in FY2025. Azure alone passed **$75B**, growing **34%**.
- **More Personal Computing** — Windows, Xbox/gaming, Surface devices, Bing/advertising. **$54.6B** in FY2025.

Total revenue was **$281.7B** with net profit of **$101.8B** in FY2025. Most of this is now
recurring subscription and cloud revenue, which is sticky and predictable.

## Why it has a moat (a wide one)
- **Switching costs:** companies run their entire operations on Microsoft software (email, documents, identity, databases). Moving off is painful and expensive.
- **Network effects:** Office, Teams, and LinkedIn get more useful as more people use them.
- **Scale in cloud:** Azure's size lets Microsoft spread huge data-center costs over a vast customer base.
- **AI distribution:** Microsoft can push AI ("Copilot", Azure AI, the OpenAI partnership) directly into a billion-plus existing seats.

I rate the moat **wide** — durable and hard to attack.

## Fair value
Blended two ways, with the code doing the arithmetic:
- **P/E:** $13.64 EPS × 28 / 32 / 36 — multiples a wide-moat compounder like MSFT has historically earned. → $382 / $436 / $491.
- **FCF yield:** $9.59 FCF/share at 3.5% / 3.0% / 2.5% yields. FCF is temporarily depressed by heavy AI capex, so this method is deliberately conservative. → $274 / $320 / $384.

**Blended fair-value band: low $328 / base $378 / high $437.**

At **$393.54**, the price sits just **above** our base case. **Margin of safety ≈ -4%** — we
would be paying full price today. Verdict: *fair, leaning expensive.* A wonderful business, but
not on sale.

## The real risks (as hard as the bull case)
- **Valuation:** no margin of safety at today's price; downside if growth disappoints.
- **AI capex is exploding** — from $44.5B (FY2024) to $64.6B (FY2025). If AI demand underwhelms, returns on that spend could be poor and FCF growth stalls.
- **Azure deceleration** as the base gets huge and AWS/Google compete hard.
- **Regulation/antitrust** in the US and EU (cloud bundling, the OpenAI relationship).
- **Macro IT-spending slowdown** would hit cloud consumption and seat growth.

## Sell triggers (the rules we'll follow)
1. Total revenue declines year-over-year for **two consecutive fiscal years**.
2. Azure / Intelligent Cloud growth falls **below 10%** for **two consecutive quarters**.
3. Company-wide gross margin falls **below 60%** for **two consecutive quarters**.
4. Free cash flow turns **negative for a full year**, OR net-debt/EBITDA rises **above 2.0x**.
5. ROIC falls **below 15%** on a sustained multi-year basis.
6. A serious accounting restatement or material governance red flag.

## Bottom line
MSFT is a wide-moat, net-cash, ~36% ROIC compounder with $71.6B of free cash flow — exactly the
kind of business this portfolio wants to own. The only thing missing today is price: at $393.54
there is no margin of safety. Conviction **4/5** — high-quality, but wait for a pullback toward
the **$328 low band** (or below) before adding aggressively.
