# Coca-Cola (KO) — Research Thesis
*As of 2026-Q1 data · written 2026-06-27 · refresh due 2026-09-25 · thesis_id KO-2026Q2*

## What the business does (plain English)
Coca-Cola is the world's biggest soft-drink company. Its clever trick is that it mostly does
**not** make the actual bottles and cans you buy. Instead it makes the secret **concentrate
(syrup base)** and sells it to a network of independent **bottlers** around the world, who add
the water and fizz, package it, and deliver it to stores. Coca-Cola owns the **brands** (Coke,
Diet Coke, Sprite, Fanta, Minute Maid, Powerade, smartwater, fairlife, Costa) and collects
high-margin concentrate money, while the heavy, low-margin bottling work sits on other
companies' balance sheets. This is why ~61 cents of every sales dollar is gross profit.
FY2024 sales were **$47.1B**.

## Why it has a moat (rated: WIDE)
The Coca-Cola trademark is one of the most valuable consumer brands on earth. Combined with a
global distribution system no one can cheaply replicate and decades-deep bottler relationships,
this gives Coca-Cola **pricing power** — in FY2024, organic revenue grew 5% mostly on
price/mix, not just volume. That durability across recessions is the hallmark of a wide moat.

## The key numbers (all cited in the thesis JSON)
- **Revenue:** $43.0B (2022) → $45.8B (2023) → $47.1B (2024); ~4.6% 3-yr CAGR. *(SEC FY2024 release; 2022 from stockanalysis.com)*
- **Gross margin:** 61.06% (FY2024). *(SEC FY2024 release, computed)*
- **ROIC:** 10.55% (FY2024). *(stockanalysis.com)*
- **Net debt / EBITDA:** ~2.86x (total debt $44.5B − cash/ST inv $12.8B = $31.7B net debt; EBITDA ~$11.1B). *(SEC FY2024 release, computed)*
- **Free cash flow:** positive. GAAP FCF $4.74B in FY2024, but that was depressed by a one-time ~$6B IRS tax-litigation deposit; the company's reported FCF **excluding** that deposit was **$10.8B**. *(SEC FY2024 release)*
- **EPS:** GAAP diluted $2.46 (FY2024, hit by one-time charges); TTM ~$3.18; FY2026 comparable EPS guided to grow 8-9% off a ~$3 2025 base. *(SEC releases; stockanalysis.com)*
- **Dividend:** 54 straight years of increases (~$2.04 in 2025, ~2.5% yield). *(stockanalysis.com)*
- **Q1 2026:** net revenues +12% to $12.47B, comparable EPS +18% to $0.86 — beat estimates. *(SEC Q1 2026 release)*

## Fair value
Price (Alpaca, 2026-06-27): **$82.61** (above its 50-day $79.33 and 200-day $74.03 averages; +17.85% over 6 months).

We blended two methods:
- **P/E:** normalized comparable EPS ~$3.10 × 22 / 25 / 28x (KO's historical range) → $68 / $78 / $87.
- **FCF yield:** normalized FCF/share ~$2.50 at 4.2 / 3.8 / 3.4% yields → $60 / $66 / $74.

**Blended fair-value band: $63.86 (low) / $71.64 (base) / $80.16 (high).**

At $82.61 the price is **slightly above** the top of that band — margin of safety roughly
**-15%**. KO is a wonderful business, but after a strong run it is fully valued to modestly
expensive today. That is why **conviction = 3**: high quality, no price cushion.

## The real risks
- **No margin of safety** at today's price.
- **Health/regulation:** sugar taxes, labeling, and GLP-1 weight-loss drugs could slow volumes.
- **Currency:** most revenue is overseas; a strong dollar dents reported results.
- **Debt:** 2.86x net-debt/EBITDA is moderate, not low; interest expense was $1.66B in 2024.
- **IRS tax litigation:** a real contingent liability; a large adverse cash resolution would hurt.
- **Maturity:** mid-single-digit growth means returns lean on price/mix, buybacks, and dividends.

## Sell triggers (what would break the thesis)
1. Gross margin below 55% for two straight quarters.
2. Net debt/EBITDA above 3.5x for more than two quarters.
3. Organic (currency-neutral) revenue down YoY for two straight quarters.
4. Dividend cut or frozen (breaks the 54-year streak).
5. Serious accounting red flag or a materially adverse, large-cash IRS resolution.
6. Price >30% above our $71.64 base case with no matching rise in earnings power.
