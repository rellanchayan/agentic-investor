## Lessons

1. Patience is a position. On the first morning of a new account with $1,000,000 in settled cash, doing nothing was the right call. Every name we have a thesis on was trading above fair value, some by a wide margin. A missed buy costs nothing; an overpriced buy locks in a bad entry that can take years to recover. Holding cash when nothing is cheap is a deliberate strategy, not an oversight.

2. The entry price is part of the thesis. Having a good thesis on MSFT or V is necessary but not sufficient. The margin of safety requirement exists precisely so that even a small error in the fair value estimate does not mean buying into a loss. The CAUTIOUS posture's 18% discount requirement is the right filter: it asks the market to do some of the work before we commit capital.

3. Know the watchlist entries before the open, not during it. The morning decision pre-calculated the buy-eligible prices (MSFT ~$310, V ~$243, AAPL ~$160 plus a conviction upgrade). Having those levels ready means tomorrow morning is a simple check — current price versus a pre-set threshold — rather than a fresh judgment under time pressure.
