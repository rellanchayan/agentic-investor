   ahead, purely because the market sold off and we held nothing. That feels good, but it
   obscures the real question: are any of the three names with live theses (MSFT, V, AAPL) now

   margin-of-safety bar? The 2026-07-14 morning decision must answer this explicitly with fresh
   prices, not just carry forward the "still too expensive" conclusion from this morning.

2. **A morning decision file written before the open is what good process looks like.** The
   2026-07-10 journal warned that missing morning files make it impossible to distinguish
   disciplined patience from passive inaction. Today there is no ambiguity: the decision was
   written, the reasoning was explicit, the prices were recorded, and the outcome matched the
   plan. That is the standard. Every trading day needs this file.

3. **The equity history JSONL gap is a future measurement risk.** Right now there is no running
   time-series of daily equity. `state/runs/` gives us snapshots but they are not aggregated.
   Once we start holding positions and earning (or losing) unrealized P&L, we will want the
   Sharpe ratio, max drawdown trajectory, and equity curve — none of which can be calculated
   without the time series. Fix this before the first position is taken, not after.
