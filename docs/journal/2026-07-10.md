## Lessons

1. SPY crossed into positive territory since our inception date while we hold all cash. The
   gap is small now (+0.58 pp in SPY's favour), but it will widen on every day the market
   rises and we own nothing. Patience is correct when nothing passes the margin-of-safety
   test; sitting in cash is not free when prices run. The discipline check for next week must
   explicitly answer: did any of the three names with live theses (MSFT, V, AAPL) come within
   our buy threshold on any of the five days we missed?

2. Five trading days passed with no journal. The 2026-07-03 journal identified this exact
   pattern and called it a documented habit rather than a learned lesson. That assessment was
   correct — the pattern continued unchanged. Recording a failure without fixing its root
   cause is not accountability; it is record-keeping. A structural trigger (not manual effort)
   is required to make the end-of-day phase reliable.

3. A missing decision file creates an unfillable gap in the audit trail. Even if we know the
   outcome (nothing traded), we cannot know whether the morning review was done and found
   nothing actionable, or whether it was never done at all. These two situations call for
   different responses. Without the file, the distinction is permanently lost.
