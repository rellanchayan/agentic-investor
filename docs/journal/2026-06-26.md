## Lessons

1. Four consecutive trading days without a morning decision file is no longer a pattern —
   it is a structural failure of the daily cycle. The PLAYBOOK requires a
   `docs/decisions/<date>.md` every trading day, even if the conclusion is "hold, no
   orders." Writing that rule has not caused it to be followed. The morning phase script
   (`code/run_phase.sh morning`) should gate on the existence of this file before the end-
   of-day phase is allowed to proceed, or the orchestrating agent must enforce it before
   marking a day complete.

2. Theses exist for AAPL, MSFT, and V. SPY has fallen 2.83% since inception, meaning
   quality names are likely cheaper today than on day one. The case for patience is real
   only when someone has checked the prices against the thresholds this morning and made a
   deliberate decision to pass. Without a decision file, we cannot tell whether today's
   inaction was discipline or drift. The journal cannot supply what the morning process
   failed to produce.

3. The widening "outperformance" gap (+2.83 pp vs SPY) must not be misread as evidence
   that holding cash is the right strategy. It reflects a declining market meeting an
   inactive account. A quality investor aims to be invested in wonderful businesses at
   fair prices, not to outperform by never investing. At some point, continued inaction
   stops being patience and starts being failure to execute the strategy at all.
