## Lessons

1. Process continuity matters more than any single day's decision. The morning decision
   file ran today for the first time since June 19. That is the right habit. The journal
   must reinforce it: a morning cycle that runs and concludes "hold" is still a cycle that
   ran. The test is whether it runs consistently, not whether it produces a trade.

2. SPY's move from -0.76% to +0.09% since inception happened in a single session. We were
   "ahead" for three weeks by holding cash during a market decline, and that advantage
   reversed in one day. This is the cost of sitting in cash while the market rises: the
   gap can close fast. The answer is not to panic-buy — paying 60% above fair value for
   AAPL because SPY moved up 0.85% would be a much worse outcome than trailing by 0.09 pp.
   The answer is to keep the buy list ready so that when a name reaches fair value, the
   decision can be made quickly.

3. Two specific price levels deserve immediate attention this week: July 9 (tariff decision)
   could create a sharp move in either direction, and July 14 (CPI print) could reprice rate
   expectations. If tariffs bite and the market sells off, MSFT at ~$330 becomes a
   serious candidate. The thesis is already written. The constitution check is ready to run.
   The only thing missing is the price. Stay patient, stay ready.
