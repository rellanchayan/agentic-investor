## Lessons

1. Event days most need a decision file. The July 7 decision explicitly named today's FOMC
   minutes as a potential market-moving event. No morning record was produced for today. That
   means the event produced signal — price movement, a read on Fed posture, a shift in the
   macro backdrop — and none of it was captured in writing. An observed event with no record
   is indistinguishable from an unobserved one.

2. The SPY gap is a clock, not a score. The 0.71 pp "outperformance" is not the product of
   a buy decision; it is the product of not being invested when SPY fell. It was 0.76 pp on
   July 3 and is 0.71 pp today — it has already started to close. The moment SPY recovers to
   its June 16 baseline, our total return matches SPY's at zero, and 15 days of disciplined
   cash-holding will have earned nothing relative to the index.

3. Tomorrow (July 9) is a named binary event. The July 6 and July 7 decision files both
   flagged the July 9 tariff cliff explicitly. A morning decision file on that day is not
   optional — it is the only way to document whether volatility moved any name into buy range,
   whether posture shifted, and what the account's considered response was. Missing it would
   mark the third consecutive end-of-day cycle with no run summary, and the second event day
   in a row with no written morning record.
