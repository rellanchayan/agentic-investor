## Lessons

1. A missing morning decision file is a gap in the record. The journal relies on `docs/decisions/<date>.md` to explain what we planned. When that file does not exist, the journal cannot reconstruct intent — it can only report the outcome (nothing happened). Going forward, the morning cycle must produce a decision document every trading day, even if the conclusion is "hold everything, place no orders."

2. SPY falling while we hold cash is not a win — it is an accident. We are ahead of SPY by 1.27 percentage points purely because we own nothing and SPY dropped. This lead will reverse the moment markets recover and we are still sitting uninvested. The goal is not to avoid losses by hiding in cash forever; it is to buy wonderful businesses at sensible prices and compound over years. Cash is a temporary waiting position, not a strategy.

3. Fed event days are not days to be reckless, but they are also not days to freeze the entire process. If the market sold off today and pushed one of our watchlist names toward its buy-eligible level, we should have been ready to act. Pre-set limit prices mean the market does the triggering for us — we do not need to make a real-time judgment on Fed day. The watchlist levels calculated yesterday exist precisely so that a volatile day becomes a mechanical check, not a paralysis.
