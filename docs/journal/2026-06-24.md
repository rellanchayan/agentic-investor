## Lessons

1. Four of five trading days have no morning decision document. This is not a pattern that
   will self-correct. Either the morning phase is not running at all on most days, or it is
   running but not producing the required output. Both are process failures. The fix is
   structural: the morning phase script should fail visibly and loudly when no decision file
   is written, not just log a warning that gets ignored.

2. Nine calendar days in, the account has placed zero trades. Holding cash because no name
   meets the margin-of-safety threshold is a perfectly defensible strategy — but only if the
   prices were actually checked each morning and confirmed to be above threshold. Without the
   morning records, there is no evidence the check happened. The account is indistinguishable
   from one that is simply not operating.

3. The cash "outperformance" vs SPY (now +2.30 pp) will compound the temptation to
   continue doing nothing. It should not. The mandate is to own a few wonderful businesses
   at sensible prices, not to hold cash indefinitely. If the watchlist names are all above
   their entry prices, the weekly research session should be expanding the watchlist. There
   is no record that a weekly research session ran on 2026-06-21. That session, and the
   morning process, need to run on their scheduled days.
