   recorded the closure in one line and deferred all review to the next open (Monday 2026-06-22).
   No action is needed, but Monday's morning review should cover the full week — four days of
   price movement (Tuesday through Friday last week, plus any overnight weekend developments)
   will have accumulated. The morning agent should be given enough context to review where MSFT,
   V, AAPL, and any scouted names stand relative to our buy thresholds before placing any order.

2. Four days in, the account has still not made a single trade. The watchlist targets set on
   2026-06-16 (MSFT near $310, V near $243, AAPL with a conviction upgrade near $160) remain
   far above current prices. If those levels still cannot be justified at Monday's open, the
   weekly research session (due Saturday 2026-06-21) is the right place to ask whether the
   watchlist needs new names that are closer to fair value today — not to lower our standards,
   but to look harder for quality businesses that the market has priced more reasonably.

3. A holiday in the middle of a short week (Monday-Wednesday last week, holiday Thursday, no
   entry Friday due to no decision file) means the first real full week of operation is still
   ahead. The team should treat Monday 2026-06-22 as a clean start: run the full morning cycle,
   produce a proper decision document, and make a deliberate choice — buy, watchlist, or
   consciously hold — before the market opens.
