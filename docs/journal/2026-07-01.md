## Lessons

1. Absence of a record is not evidence of absence of a reason. On any given day that
   the morning cycle fails to produce a decision file, the journal can only report
   "nothing happened" — it cannot confirm that the decision to hold cash was deliberate
   and correctly reasoned. A consciously held cash position is just as valid as a
   consciously held equity position; the difference is the word "consciously." The
   missing decision file removes that word. The morning cycle must write a decision
   document every trading day, even if the document is one sentence: "All three
   watchlist names remain above their cautious-posture entry prices. No orders placed."

2. SPY's partial recovery narrows the one metric that makes the account look good.
   Between June 22 and July 1, SPY recovered approximately 0.19 percentage points of
   its decline. If SPY continues to recover without the account buying into any position,
   the reported outperformance will converge to zero and then go negative — and the
   account will have sat in cash through an entire market recovery with no thesis-backed
   positions to benefit from it. Patient waiting for a margin of safety is correct;
   waiting indefinitely because the morning cycle is not running is not patience, it is
   inaction. The distinction matters.

3. The weekly research session (due each Saturday) should be expanding or refreshing the
   watchlist. The three theses on file (AAPL, MSFT, V) were all written on 2026-06-16.
   No research session output appears in `docs/` for the weekends of June 21 or June 28.
   If no new names have been scouted and no existing theses have been updated, the watch-
   list is stale. A name that was far above fair value on June 16 may have pulled back
   into range by now — or a new name not yet on the list may be at a compelling price.
   Without updated research, there is no way to know, and the account will continue to
   sit uninvested by default rather than by decision.
