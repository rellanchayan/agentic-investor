## Lessons

1. The missing morning decision file is now a pattern, not a one-off. Three of the five
   trading days in the account's life (2026-06-17, 2026-06-18, and 2026-06-22) have no
   decision record. A single lesson in the PLAYBOOK has not fixed the habit. The morning phase
   must be enforced structurally — either the `code/run_phase.sh morning` script should exit
   non-zero if no decision file is written, or the journal should flag it as a process failure
   (as it does here). Logging the rule is not the same as following it.

2. Five days in, the account has made no trades and holds only cash. That is a defensible
   outcome if every name on the watchlist genuinely failed the margin-of-safety test this
   morning — but without a decision file, we cannot confirm this. The weekly research session
   (due Saturday 2026-06-21, one day ago) was an opportunity to either expand the watchlist or
   confirm that cash remains the right answer. There is no record of whether that session ran.
   If it did not, the account risks drifting — not by trading badly, but by failing to engage
   with the process at all.

3. Our "outperformance" vs SPY widens every day SPY falls while we hold cash. This creates a
   subtle psychological trap: the longer we wait, the more our uninvested cash looks like a
   good decision. It is not a good decision — it is the absence of a decision. A real quality
   investor in cash because no name meets the threshold is being disciplined; an investor in
   cash because the morning process did not run is just inactive. The journal cannot tell the
   difference from the data alone. Only a written morning record could.
