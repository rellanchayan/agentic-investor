## Lessons

1. A gap this long is not a process slip — it is a process failure. Five trading days is a
   full week. Seven consecutive days without a morning decision or end-of-day journal means
   the entire operating cycle stopped. The account is on autopilot (cash) and not being managed.
   The prior journal flagged this as a pattern after three missed days. We are now at eleven.
   Flagging it in a journal entry has not fixed it. Something structural needs to change: either
   the morning and EOD scripts are scheduled to run automatically, or there is a hard external
   reminder that fires when the decision file is missing by 10:00 ET. Writing it down one more
   time is not enough.

2. The portfolio history confirms the EOD snapshot script was not running either. This is
   important: we are not just missing the human-written analysis — we are missing the automated
   data collection too. If the automation that requires no judgment is also not running, the
   problem is with the execution environment, not with decision quality. That is a different
   problem to fix.

3. "Cash outperforming SPY" is the easiest false comfort in investing. When the market falls
   and you are in cash, you look smart. When it recovers, you look inactive. Neither is skill.
   The only way to know whether holding cash was right is to have a written record showing that
   specific names failed specific price tests on specific mornings. We have no such record for
   the last seven trading days. We cannot claim the cash was a disciplined decision. It was
   simply inertia.
