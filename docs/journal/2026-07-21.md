## Lessons

1. **Leading SPY by holding cash is not investing — it is waiting.** The 0.32-point lead over
   SPY since inception exists because SPY fell while we sat in cash. That is capital preservation,
   not alpha. If SPY recovers, we will give back the lead without having earned anything. The only
   way to actually beat SPY is to own something that outperforms it. Thirty-five calendar days
   in, we have never owned a share. The risk of permanent underperformance from never buying is
   just as real as the risk of buying poorly.

2. **Five consecutive trading days with no morning write-up is a meaningful failure.** The audit
   trail between 2026-07-14 and today is a blank. Whether the cautious posture from 2026-07-13
   remained correct across that week — whether MSFT dipped to a buyable price, whether the
   geopolitical backdrop shifted, whether the FOMC meeting (scheduled July 28-29) changed the
   forward picture — none of that can be assessed now. The whole point of the morning decision
   file is to make the reasoning visible and reviewable. Without it, the system is not learning;
   it is just drifting.

3. **The next morning review must answer a specific question: has anything changed since July 13?**
   The three items the 2026-07-13 decision said to watch were: (a) the US-Iran/oil situation,
   (b) the macro calendar (CPI, Warsh testimony, PPI, FOMC July 28-29), and (c) whether MSFT
   fell to ~$333 or below (normal posture) or ~$310 or below (cautious posture). Tomorrow's
   morning write-up should address each of those directly, with current prices, before deciding
   whether to maintain or relax the cautious posture.
