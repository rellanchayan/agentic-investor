## Lessons

1. The morning decision cycle must run every trading day and produce a `docs/decisions/<date>.md`
   file, even when the conclusion is simply "hold, place no orders." Three trading days in, we
   have only one decision document (2026-06-16). Days 2 and 3 have no record of what was observed
   or decided that morning. This is a discipline failure in the process, not a trading failure —
   but an honest journal must name it. If a future review asks why we held cash on 2026-06-17 and
   2026-06-18, there is no written answer.

2. SPY recovered roughly 0.76 percentage points today. Our advantage over SPY since inception
   is narrowing (from +1.27 pp yesterday to +0.51 pp today) purely because we are uninvested
   while the market moves. This is the cost of patience: when the market falls, cash looks
   smart; when the market recovers, cash looks slow. The goal is not to have the highest cash
   return — it is to buy at sensible prices and compound over years. The watchlist targets
   (MSFT ~$310, V ~$243) are the discipline. We wait for those levels, not for market direction.

3. Three days in, the account is functionally a savings account. That is acceptable for a short
   period when no name meets the margin of safety threshold, but it will not remain acceptable
   indefinitely. If prices do not come down to us, the team will need to revisit either the
   watchlist (add new names that may be closer to fair value) or the posture (if the cautious
   bump of 6% is warranted given updated macro conditions). Standing still is a choice — it
   should be a conscious, reviewed choice, not a default.
