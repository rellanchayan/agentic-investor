## Lessons

1. A single PLAYBOOK entry about missing morning decision files has not changed the pattern.
   Seven consecutive trading days passed without a journal. The lesson learned on 2026-06-22
   was "log the failure." The failure continued anyway. Logging is not a fix. The process needs
   a structural enforcement mechanism — something that cannot be silently skipped. Until that
   mechanism exists, the journal will keep recording the same failure.

2. The account is now ten trading days old and has made zero investments. That is a defensible
   outcome only if every watchlist name failed the margin-of-safety test every single day. The
   2026-06-16 decision document shows AAPL at $297, MSFT at $393, V at $329 — all well above
   their buy-eligible prices. Whether those prices moved in the right direction during the gap
   is unknown from this record. If any name dipped toward its threshold during the five missing
   days and we were not there to observe it, that was a real cost — not a visible loss, but an
   opportunity the process was not in position to take.

3. The widening cash-vs-SPY gap is not a reason to delay investing further. SPY down 1.29%
   means our cash "outperforms" by 1.29 pp. This number will shrink or reverse as soon as
   markets recover, and it provides zero compounding in the meantime. A quality investor holds
   cash because no name meets the threshold today. An inactive investor holds cash because the
   morning process did not run. This account has been the second kind for most of its life.
   The distinction matters.
