## Lessons

1. **Leading SPY for the wrong reason is not a win.** We now show +0.97pp over SPY since
   inception, but this is a byproduct of cash safety during a brief market pullback, not
   evidence of skill. The moment SPY recovers, that lead vanishes — and we still hold no
   shares, so we will miss the rebound exactly as we missed the prior run-up. The true test of
   whether cash was the right call is whether any of the three live-thesis names (AAPL, MSFT, V)
   traded through our margin-of-safety threshold during the pullback. The record does not answer
   that, because no morning reviews were written.

2. **Four consecutive trading days with no morning decision file is a systemic failure, not a
   one-off gap.** The daily cycle requires a written morning record every trading day — even when
   the answer is "hold, nothing changed." Without it, the cautious posture persists by inertia,
   not by deliberate choice reviewed against current prices and the live macro context. That is
   not patience; it is drift. The next morning session must produce a written decision file, even
   if the conclusion is identical to 2026-07-13.

3. **A declining market is exactly when margin-of-safety discipline must be actively applied,
   not passively waited out.** If the market fell ~1.15pp in three days, individual quality
   names likely fell further. The margin-of-safety gap that existed on 2026-07-13 (MSFT was
   closest, at 2% above base with an 18% threshold required) may have narrowed or closed. The
   purpose of holding cash is to deploy it when prices become fair. We cannot know whether that
   moment arrived and passed unnoticed during the last four days.
